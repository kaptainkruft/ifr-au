IFR Phraseology Australia - Simple Reference

for use on VATSIM only

Examples on this page assume *all* positions are online. This is rarely the case. Simply continue your comms with the lowest controller above you: Delivery, Ground, Tower, Approach, Enroute.

YMML to YSSY

In this example, John is flying an Airbus A320 from Melbourne to Sydney, and expects to be in controlled airspace throughout the flight. Callsign: JST520. Route: DOSEL Y59 RIVET. This is standard airliner fodder.
John fires up his simulator, generates and files a flight plan, and begins virtually loading passengers. When he's about fifteen minutes away from being ready to depart, he contacts clearance delivery on 127.2Mhz.
Because John has already checked the ATIS, he knows the in-use runway, and therefore which departure to use (for the waypoint he has planned), and any other information that is pertinent.
If delivery was not online, he would contact the first online of; Ground, Tower, Approach, Center.

JST520 John Smith
Melbourne delivery, Jetstar five twenty, request clearance through to Sydney.

ML_DEL Melbourne delivery
Jetstar five twenty, melbourne delivery, G'day.
Cleared to Sydney via DOSEL flight planned route, DOSEL1 departure, runway 27, climb via SID 5000, squawk 5763, departures on 132.0.

JST520 John Smith
Cleared to Sydney via DOSEL flight planned route, DOSEL1 departure runway 27, climb via the SID to 5000, squawk 5763, departures on 132.0.

ML_DEL Melbourne delivery
Jetstar five twenty. When ready contact ground on 121.7. G'day.

JST520 John Smith
Ground on 121.7. G'day

 
Once all John's passengers are loaded and he is ready to go, he contacts checks the ATIS again and then Ground for clearance to start up and push back.

JST520 John Smith
Melbourne ground, Jetstar five twenty type Airbus A320, stand echo six, in receipt of golf, request pushback.

ML_GND Melbourne ground
Jetstar five twenty, Melbourne ground, g'day. Pushback approved.

JST520 John Smith
Pushback approved, Jetstar 520.

 
Once John is pushed back and facing west, engines ablaze, taxi checklists complete everyone set and ready to go, he gets a pencil ready to copy the taxi clearance and contacts ground again.

JST520 John Smith
Jetstar five twenty, request taxi.

ML_GND Melbourne ground
Jetstar five twenty, taxi via golf, alpha, echo, to holding point papa, runway two seven.

JST520 John Smith
Taxi via golf, alpha, echo, to holding point papa runway two seven, Jetstar five twenty.

 
John taxies out of the apron on to the manoeuvring area (taxiways) and proceeds west on golf, right on to alpha, right on to echo, and left to hold short of 27 at papa.
In Australia, once you reach the holding point (or reasonably close that you are not likely to be needed by ground), you are expected to swap frequencies to tower automatically yourself.
John completes all the pre-takeoff checklists and then tunes the tower frequency, 120.5, and contacts tower to call ready - which indicates that the aircraft is completely ready to depart immediately.

JST520 John Smith
Melbourne tower, Jetstar five twenty, holding point papa runway two seven, ready.

ML_TWR Melbourne tower
Jetstar five twenty, Melbourne tower, g'day. Winds one two zero degrees at two five knots, runway two seven cleared for take off.

JST520 John Smith
Runway two seven, cleared for takeoff, Jetstar five twenty.

 
John moves on to the runway and lines up, applies take off thrust and departs. A few moments after passing the opposite end of the runway...

ML_TWR Melbourne tower
Jetstar five twenty, contact departures, g'day.

JST520 John Smith
Departures, one three two decimal zero, Jetstar five twenty.

 
John tunes approach on 132.0. While making his departure call, he notes his altitude to the nearest one hundred feet. Note! If you are on VATSIM and you're with the same controller (i.e. approach or center) you MUST still make your departure call!

JST520 John Smith
Melbourne departures, Jetstar five twenty, climbing through three thousand six hundred for five thousand.
Note: While on VATSIM you will talk to approach almost always, it will help the controller know you are departing and not just randomly calling from somewhere out of the blue.

ML_APP Melbourne approach
Jetstar five twenty, Melbourne approach, G'day. Identified. Climb via the SID flight level two four zero.

JST520 John Smith
Climb via the SID, flight level two four zero, Jetstar five twenty.

 
After climbing through the terminal area, John either approaches the vertical boundary (FL240) or the lateral boundary (about 1/2 - 2/3 to DOSEL), approach contacts him.

ML_APP Melbourne approach
Jetstar five twenty, contact Melbourne center on one two three decimal seven five. G'day.

JST520 John Smith
One two three decimal seven five for Jetstar five twenty. G'day.

 
John tunes 123.75, eyeballs his altimiter to the nearest hundred feet and contacts center.

JST520 John Smith
Melbourne center, Jetstar five twenty, passing flight level two two three for two four zero.

ML_CTR Melbourne center
Jetstar five twenty, Melbourne center, g'day. Climb flight level three seven zero.

JST520 John Smith
Climb flight level three seven zero, Jetstar five twenty.

 
As the flight progresses, John may get handed off to various controllers and the flow is generally the same.

UNIT center
Jetstar five twenty, contact UNIT. on FREQUENCY. G'day.

JST520 John Smith
FREQUENCY for Jetstar five twenty, g'day.

JST520 John Smith
UNIT Jetstar five twenty, flight level three seven zero, g'day.

UNIT center
Jetstar five twenty, G'day.

 
Eventually the time comes for descent, arrival and approach. A controller will either offer you a STAR, or tell you to expect one with a hand off. If so, they'll simply add "Expect STAR clearance" with the handoff. There is no need to acknowledge the expectation.

ML_CTR Melbourne center
Jetstar five twenty, STAR clearance available.

 
John grabs a pencil, and prepares to jot down the details.

JST520 John Smith
Ready to copy, Jetstar five twenty.

ML_CTR Melbourne center
Jetstar five twenty, cleared the RIVET three arrival, runway one six right. When ready, descend to flight level one three zero.

JST520 John Smith
RIVET three arrival runway one six right, when ready descend flight level one three zero, Jetstar five twenty.

 
Don't decend immediately! It's very annoying to controllers when you drop out of the sky and then crawl along at relatively low speed. It ruins their sequencing, makes your flight longer, and is suboptimal for everyone.
John consults his calculations and flight management computer to determine the top of descent and waits until shortly before reaching it. Then he enters the cleared level, FL130, and engages the VNAV profile.

JST520 John Smith
Jetstar five twenty, leaving flight level three seven zero.
Note: This isn't strictly necessary, but it is a politeness so a controller will know you're now on the way down. Omit this on a busy day like milkrun to keep the channel clear.

ML_CTR Melbourne center
Jetstar five twenty.

 
As John approaches various airspace boundaries, or the assigned altitude, center will issue further clearances. He simply reads back each clearance. The last descent clearance usually includes the local QNH once it is below the transition layer.

ML_CTR Melbourne center
Jetstar five twenty, descend via the STAR to niner thousand. Sydney QNH one zero one three.

JST520 John Smith
Down to niner thousand, QNH one zero one three. Jetstar five twenty.

 
Eventually the time comes for John to be handed to Sydney approach.

ML_CTR Melbourne center
Jetstar five twenty, contact Sydney approach on one two four decimal four. G'day.

JST520 John Smith
One two four decimal four, Jetstar five twenty.

 
John double checks that he has the ATIS for Sydney, notes its code, tunes approach and contacts them.

JST520 John Smith
Sydney approach, Jetstar five twenty, descending through flight level one one zero for niner thousand, we have romeo.

SY_APP Sydney approach
Jetstar five twenty, Sydney approach, g'day. Runway one six right, expect ILS approach.

JST520 John Smith
One six right and expecting the ILS, Jetstar five twenty.

 
Another series of descent instructions will follow as you progress through the control area. Acknowledge these. At Sydney almost all arrivals are radar vectored, so some time you will get an instruction like this:

SY_APP Sydney approach
Jetstar five twenty, descend four thousand, turn left heading three four zero.

JST520 John Smith
Descend to four thousand, turn left heading three four zero.

 
John is now being radar vectored, and is not under his own navigation. Follow ATC instructions until you are instructed either to resume your STAR (unlikely at Sydney), or cleared for an approach. After a series of headings, you will be finally turned toward the approach.

SY_APP Sydney approach
Jetstar five twenty, you are four miles west of the localizer, turn right heading one three zero. Maintain four thousand until established. Cleared the ILS runway one six right approach. Report established.

JST520 John Smith
Right one three zero, maintain four thousand until established, cleared ILS runway one six right approach, wilco. Jetstar five twenty.

JST520 John Smith
Once John is established on the localizer in approach mode
Jetstar five twenty, established one six right.

SY_APP Sydney approach
Jetstar five twenty, contact Sydney tower on one two zero decimal five. G'day.

JST520 John Smith
One two zero decimal five, Jetstar five twenty. G'day.

JST520 John Smith
John tunes 120.5, and notices there are two aircraft ahead of him.
Sydney tower, Jetstar five twenty, on the ILS runway one six right.

SY_TWR Sydney tower
Jetstar five twenty, Sydney tower, G'day, number three.

SY_TWR Sydney tower
John notices the aircraft is a bit slow off the runway in front of him.
Jetstar five twenty, Sydney tower, expect late landing clearance.

JST520 John Smith
Expect late clearance, Jetstar five twenty.

SY_TWR Sydney tower
The laggard pilot gets his aircraft out of the way as John reaches the two mile mark.
Jetstar five twenty, runway one six right, cleared to land.

JST520 John Smith
Cleared to land one six right, Jetstar five twenty.

 
John lands, and exits the runway via the high speed taxiway B7. Once he passes the holding point, he contacts tower again.

JST520 John Smith
Vacated one six right, bravo seven on to bravo, Jetstar five twenty.

SY_TWR Sydney tower
Jetstar five twenty, welcome to Sydney, contact ground on one two six decimal five. G'day.

JST520 John Smith
Slowing to a very slow taxi speed, John slowly moves northbound on Bravo
Ground on one two six decimal five, Jetstar five twenty, g'day.

JST520 John Smith
John grabs a pencil in case of anything nonstandard and tunes 126.5
Sydney ground, Jetstar five twenty on bravo northbound for domestic two.

SY_GND Sydney ground
Jetstar five twenty, Sydney ground, g'day. Taxi via bravo, cross runway two five, golf, to the apron.

JST520 John Smith
Via bravo, cross two five, golf to the apron, Jetstar five twenty.

 
John parks at bay 56, and powers down. Once he enters the apron he's no longer ground's problem. Cracks a beer and opens up simbrief to plan his next flight.
